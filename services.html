Our Services

Sea freight or ocean freight is a method of shipping goods by sea using cargo ships, and the percentage is higher for most developing countries. There are several types of sea freight, differentiated based on the size and type of goods shipped. The common types of sea freight include :

Full Container Load (FCL)

In FCL, a single shipper fills an entire container with their goods. This method is ideal for a large quantity of goods. FCL is faster and less risky than LCL.

Less Container Load (LCL)

LCL is used when the shipped goods do not fill a full container. Goods are shipped with others from different shippers in one container.

Air freight, or air cargo allows the transport of goods quickly by air. Shipping cargo by air has always been a high cost-to-weight form of shipment, countered by the fact that it’s the fastest way to transport goods internationally. There are two types of air cargo :

General Cargo

Includes high-value goods like electronics, jewelry, and pharmaceuticals.

Special Cargo

Requires special conditions for transport, such as temperature control or protected casing.

Trucking or road transportation is the most common form of land transport. It offers flexibility and door-to-door service, essential for last-mile delivery.

Railways provide a cost-effective means to move large quantities of goods over long distances, commonly used for transporting bulk commodities.

Third-party logistics (3PL) refers to the outsourcing of e-commerce logistics processes to a third-party business, including inventory management, warehousing, and fulfillment.
